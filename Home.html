Neboo
(Hadey Lewis)

Game Dev and Composer

Hadey is a programmer and musician based in San Diego, California. She started composing from the young age of 11 by throwing together and mixing loops in a free DAW. Since then, she has experimented with a vast variety of electronic compositions ranging from purely blissful ambiance to dark pulsating club music. Using the ideas from all of these genres, she has built a style that masterfully combines them into a singular creative motif that resonates throughout all of her work. She dreams of using her creative knowledge and love of the games industry in particular to realize new creative ​visions and push the ​boundaries of what a game's OST can be. With deep layers of dynamically shifting textures, she aims to immerse the player ​not only in the game world, but also in the music as well.

Even before she started experimenting with music, she taught herself c, c++, c#, gdscript and (mostly) python. She now programs stuff every once in a while whenever she has ideas, or whenever her game dev club needs her.

But just about 2 years ago, she discovered 3D art with Blender and After Effects to make music videos. Since then she has made visual representations for most of her work, helping relay the feeling she was originally trying to communicate with the piece. She also has started dabbling with drawing 2D art occasionally.

Hadey runs a game development group called Digital Arts and Code, which is a 40 member group of high-school and college aged students that use their different skills to work together on various game and other multimedia projects. She acts as the producer and manages the development of games from the ground up, asking the right questions and making sure everyone is on the same page. She also makes sure to help out with audio, as well as code when necessary.

Another function of the Digital Arts and Code club is to teach people about their chosen skills. Members of the club who are now majoring in art, machine learning, and composition also mentor younger members.  They help to provide an environment where less expirenced club members can share their progress and get feedback and advice from those who are more experienced. In this way, the Digital Arts enables everyone to grow as artists.

Have a lovely day!
